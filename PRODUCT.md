# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International development practitioners, research institutions, government agencies, and programme leads who need a rigorous data scientist for evidence work. They arrive already knowing who Johnson is — from a referral, a publication, or a conference — and come to confirm that the work is as serious as the reputation. Context: they are evaluating collaborators, not browsing freelancers.

Secondary: academic hiring committees and research programme officers at organisations like USAID, UNDP, CGIAR, World Bank, and African policy institutes.

## Product Purpose

A professional portfolio that establishes Johnson Mugarra as a published, peer-reviewed researcher and applied ML practitioner — not a freelancer seeking work. The site communicates depth, consequence, and geographic specificity. Primary goal: a peer-to-peer enquiry ("let's talk about a project"), not a "hire me" transaction.

Success: a visitor reads the about section, notes the publications, reviews one project, and reaches out with a clear problem to solve.

## Brand Personality

Measured. Consequential. Field-tested.

Not ambitious-startup. Not academic-dry. The quiet confidence of someone who has presented findings to a head of state and compressed six-week evaluation cycles to ten days, and doesn't need to announce either fact loudly.

Voice: direct, specific, no buzzwords. Numbers are earned, not decorative. The writing sounds like a researcher who also ships.

## Anti-references

- SaaS/startup landing pages (gradients, glassmorphism, floating dashboards, glowing orbs)
- Generic "AI tool" portfolios (purple-blue gradients, feature grids, hero metrics template)
- Vague academic sites (walls of text, no visual hierarchy, zero imagery)
- African-pattern clichés (kente / tribal motifs as aesthetic decoration — the cultural identity comes through geography and specificity, not pattern)
- Editorial-magazine typographic lane (display serif italic + mono labels + ruled broadsheet grid): saturated lane, wrong register
- "Hire me" transactional framing anywhere on the page

## Design Principles

1. **Earned numbers only.** Every metric shown must have a story. No decorative stat blocks.
2. **Geography is identity.** Johnson's work is East Africa. That specificity — Kampala, satellite data, vanilla exports, MAAIF — is the differentiator. Surface it, don't bury it.
3. **Researcher first, for-hire second.** Publications, presented research, institutional affiliations lead. The contact form follows naturally from the credibility established before it.
4. **Quiet authority.** Never announce confidence. Let the work do it. Restraint is earned, not lazy.
5. **Specific over vague.** "Compressed evaluation cycles from 6 weeks to 10 days for USAID SITES" over "improved operational efficiency." Always.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Support dark/light mode. Prefers-reduced-motion respected. Strong contrast ratios — primary text minimum 7:1 on background. All imagery has descriptive alt text.
