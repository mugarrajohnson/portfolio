About me

Shaped by Research. Defined by the Mathematics.

I started my career in management consulting at Bronkar Uganda — learning how organisations frame decisions and what makes evidence actually move people. That curiosity pulled me deeper: I moved into M&E research for international non-profits, running evaluations and impact assessments for USAID, UNDP, IRC, and others across East Africa.

The more rigorous the questions got, the more I wanted the mathematical tools to match them. That's what drove me to the M.Sc. in Data Science — not a pivot, a deliberate deepening. I wanted to work across the full stack: machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and Bayesian statistical inference — not just apply packaged methods, but understand what's happening inside them.

That depth has shaped the systems I've built since: a predictive analytics platform for Uganda's vanilla export sector at MAAIF and Makerere University, fusing NASA POWER satellite data with ground measurements to cut forecast error by 18% — findings that reached a national forum attended by the Head of State. And automated evaluation pipelines at USAID SITES that compressed six-week assessment cycles to under ten days.

I'm drawn to problems where the uncertainty is real, the data is difficult, and getting it right actually changes something. Certified in PMP, Power BI, SAS, and Tableau. Based in Kampala — open to remote and international engagements.

Agricultural Analytics · Jan 2024 – Jun 2026

Vanilla Harvest Season Analysis

Pollination activity and projected harvest output across 1,000 Ugandan vanilla farmers (January 2024 – June 2026). Vanilla beans take approximately 9 months to mature after hand-pollination: the harvest line is the pollination curve shifted forward by 9 months. The main pollination season (September–November) drives the June–July main harvest; the secondary season (February–May) drives the December–January harvest.
